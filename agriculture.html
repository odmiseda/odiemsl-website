Sustainable Agriculture in Kenya: A 2025 Guide

Kenya’s Western Province produces over 70% of the nation’s sugarcane. Yet, erratic rainfall, soil degradation, and pests threaten yields. At Odiems Limited, we’ve partnered with agronomists to deliver **practical, field‑tested advice** for smallholder farmers.

1. Soil Health & Testing

Ideal sugarcane pH: 6.0–7.0. Use KEBS soil kits (KSh 500) available at local agrovet shops. Add lime (KSh 1,200/50kg) if pH < 6.0. Our 2024 pilot in Kakamega increased yields by 18% after pH correction.

2. Drought‑Resistant Varieties

  • CO 421: High sucrose, drought tolerant
  • Kenya 85–2000: Early maturing, pest resistant
  • KEN 82–216: Best for high altitude

3. Pest & Disease Control

Common threats: **sugarcane borer**, **smut**, **leaf scald**. Use **Imidacloprid** (KSh 800/liter) or biological controls like **Trichogramma wasps**. Rotate with legumes to break pest cycles.

4. Irrigation & Water Management

Drip irrigation costs KSh 20,000/acre but saves 60% water. Apply for AFC subsidies. Mulch with cane trash to retain moisture.

5. Harvesting & Post‑Harvest

Harvest at 12–14 months. Cut at ground level. Deliver to mill within 24 hours to preserve sucrose. Odiems offers transport at KSh 200/ton.
