Understanding Kenya's Sugarcane Landscape

Sugarcane covers over 100,000 hectares in Kenya, primarily in Western Province, contributing KSh 100 billion annually to GDP. But challenges like erratic rainfall and pests loom large. Drawing from our partnerships with local mills such as Sony Sugar Company and Mumias Sugar, here's how to thrive...

  • Variety Selection: Opt for drought-resistant hybrids like CO 421—yields 10% more in dry spells. We've seen farmers in Kakamega double outputs.
  • Soil Health: Test pH (ideal 6.0-7.0) via KEBS kits (KSh 500). Amend with lime from local quarries.

Climate-Smart Farming Tips for 2025

With El Niño fading but La Niña on the horizon (per Kenya Meteorological Department forecasts), prepare now. Drip irrigation systems—affordable at KSh 20,000/acre via subsidies from the Agricultural Finance Corporation (AFC)—can save 60% water.
